I was surprised that energy dominated results even when genre didn’t match.
This showed me how sensitive recommender systems are to weight tuning.

High-Energy Pop vs Chill Lofi:
High-Energy Pop gives fast and energetic songs, while Chill Lofi gives slow and calm songs. This makes sense because energy preference is very different.

Deep Intense Rock vs High-Energy Pop:
Both have high energy, so some songs overlap. But rock songs rank higher in the rock profile because of genre match.

Conflicting Mood vs High-Energy Pop:
Conflicting Mood still gives high energy songs, but mood is ignored because it doesn’t match. That is why results look similar but slightly worse.

No Preference vs others:
No Preference mostly gives average energy songs. It doesn’t feel personalized because genre and mood are missing.
